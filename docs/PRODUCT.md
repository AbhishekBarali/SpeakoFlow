# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SpeakoFlow is for anyone who talks to their computer. That spans privacy-conscious
power users and developers who want to own their voice stack (no subscriptions,
fully local-first) and everyday people who just want fast dictation and a helpful
spoken assistant. The common thread: they are already working inside another app
(writing, coding, browsing, messaging) and reach for SpeakoFlow through a global
hotkey without breaking flow. They expect it to stay out of the way, respect their
privacy, and feel trustworthy with their voice and their screen.

## Product Purpose

A free, local-first desktop voice assistant that replaces paid Wispr Flow-style
subscriptions. Three jobs sit under one hotkey layer:

- Dictation — local speech-to-text typed into the active app; audio never leaves the machine.
- Assistant — speak or type a question and get a streaming answer in a floating glass panel.
- Screen vision and spoken answers — optionally let the model see the screen and read replies aloud.

Success is the user trusting it enough to make it their default voice layer: it feels
instant, private, and premium enough that reaching for a paid alternative never crosses
their mind.

## Brand Personality

Calm, premium, quiet, trustworthy. The interface is a confident editorial instrument,
not a noisy dashboard. Voice is spare, precise, and reassuring. It earns trust by being
legible and unhurried, by showing restraint with color and motion, and by never shouting.
Premium is carried by typographic craft, generous space, and material quality, not by ornament.

## Anti-references

- Generic Electron/Tauri settings UIs: flat gray rows, hard borders, default system controls.
- Busy SaaS dashboards and developer-tool density: overstuffed panels, tiny dense type, chrome everywhere.
- "Configurable everything" control panels where every option carries equal weight and nothing has hierarchy.
- Saturated gamer-RGB or neon AI-app aesthetics. Color is a rare moment here, never the surface.
- AI-slop tells: uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, identical card grids, gradient text, glassmorphism as a default.

## Design Principles

1. Quiet by default, premium in the details. Restraint is the brand; craft (type, spacing, material) carries the premium feel, not decoration.
2. Hierarchy over uniformity. Every screen has one clear primary action; secondary options recede until needed. Equal-weight rows are a smell.
3. Trust through legibility. Strong contrast, calm pacing, honest states. The user must always feel safe handing over voice and screen.
4. Stay out of the way. The app is a layer over the user's real work; it should feel instant and never demand attention it has not earned.
5. One color moment. Color appears as a deliberate accent (orb glow, active state, send) against warm paper and ink, never as wall-to-wall chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text at least 4.5:1 and large text at least 3:1 in both light and
dark themes (watch the warm-muted tokens on paper, the most likely failure point). Full
keyboard operability with a visible focus ring on every control. Honor prefers-reduced-motion
(already wired). RTL and i18n across many locales are first-class. Never rely on color alone
to signal state such as the active model, recording, or errors.
